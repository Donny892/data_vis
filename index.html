The Sparesely Populated Landscape of Australia

Exploring the vast contrasts in the population distrbution of Australia

Australia is home to over 25 million people, making it 55th most populous country on Earth. It is also a very large country, being home to some 7.6 milllion square kilometers of land. This inherently makes Australia one of the least densely populated countries in the world, with just 3 people per square kilometer.

You can use your cursor to find out more about the graphics in this visualisation

Concentrated by the Coast

The image on the left depicts the concentration of the Auastralian population around the coastal areas of the country, particularly in the East. The small yellow marks indicate the urban areas which is home to 86.1% of the Australian population!

The red lines are the major highways in Australia, showing that there are vast expanses of the interior of the continent that have such small population that it doesn't even require road infrastruscture.

Zooming In

Zooming in on the state capitals, the population density can multiple orders of magnitude higher further inland. But even when zooming in, it can be observed that the population density drops off significantly the further you get from the city centre.

It is interesting to see that irrespective of the total population of each city, the population density can be found to be much higher than the sourrounding regions.

Urban Sprawl

Zooming in further on Melbourne and showing the data by each suburb, it can be seen apart from a dense CBD, there is a consistent level of population density thoroughout the metropolitan areas before falling of on the urban-fringe.

This can be attributed to many Australian's choosing to live in freestanding houses, with personal back yards. This causes the cities in Australia to be largely suburban, with the urban boundary far from the central business district.

How does Australia compare to Other Countries?

Australia no only has one of the largest land masses out of any country, it also has one of the lowest population densities despite all of that area.

The chart on the right shows a clear trend that generally, with more land surface area, countries will have a larger population.
